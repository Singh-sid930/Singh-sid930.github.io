Siddharth Singh

Software Development Engineer, Amazon.

I am currently a software development engineer at Amazon working to automate data vending solutions and sortation technologies. At Amazon I work to provide customers and consumers with real time data helping in decision making processes which streamline package sortation and delivery. Previously, I was a graduate student at University of Pennsylvania in the Electrical and Systems Engineering department and worked in the domain of General Robotics and Automation. [Curriculum Vitae]

At Penn, I worked in the GRASP lab in the group of Professor Kostas Daniilidis under the mentorship of Bernadette Bucher. My work at GRASP largely involved the exploration of curiosity and curious agents as used in not just problems relating to computer vision based exploration but also multi agent system exploration. More outlined in the research and projects section.

At Penn, I also worked at MLab in the group of Professor Rahul Mangharam under the mentorship of Matthew O' Kelly. At Mlabs I worked towards the development of perception pipeline for autonomous vehicles which are tightly integrated with the path planning algorithms. The work was done on 1/10th sized vehicles with Nvidia-Tx2 onboard the vehicle along with a multi-modal sensor suit. More outlined in the projects and research section.

In the past, I have also worked as a motion path planning intern at Robotics Research Center, Hyderabad working towards the development of full scale autonomous vehicles reaching level-3 autonomy. More outlined in the projects and research section.
