The Tide Remembers

The shore forgets, but the water does not. It carries the cold of every winter folded quiet beneath its turning.

Call it by an old name and it answers in the grey language of returning things — a gull, a hull, a hand let go too soon.

I came to leave something here. The tide took it, and gave it back, and took it again, gentler the second time.

Siddharth Singh
